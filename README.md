# saai-climate-health-intelligence
# SAAI Climate Health Intelligence Platform

AI-powered climate-health intelligence platform designed to strengthen early warning, preparedness, and resilience for vulnerable children and communities in low-resource settings.

---

# About the Project

Climate change is increasingly affecting the health and wellbeing of children across vulnerable communities in Africa. Floods, droughts, extreme heat, environmental degradation, and climate-sensitive disease outbreaks continue to place pressure on already constrained health systems.

The SAAI Climate Health Intelligence Platform is being developed to help communities, frontline health workers, and decision-makers better anticipate and respond to climate-related health risks using artificial intelligence, climate intelligence, and public health data.

The platform combines:
- climate and environmental indicators,
- disease surveillance data,
- geospatial analytics,
- and community-level reporting

to support:
- outbreak prediction,
- hotspot detection,
- vulnerability mapping,
- and operational decision-making.

The solution is designed specifically for low-resource environments and aims to strengthen resilience for vulnerable children and underserved communities.

---

# Vision

To build scalable AI-driven public-good infrastructure that strengthens climate-health resilience and supports earlier action for vulnerable populations across Africa.

---

# Key Features

## Climate-Health Prediction
AI models designed to identify emerging risks linked to climate-sensitive diseases such as:
- cholera,
- malaria,
- heat-related illness,
- and waterborne diseases.

## Geospatial Intelligence
Interactive risk mapping and hotspot analysis for climate-health surveillance.

## Frontline Decision Support
Operational insights and alerts to support timely response and resource prioritization.

## Community Reporting
Mobile and messaging-based reporting systems to strengthen local participation and situational awareness.

## Low-Connectivity Design
Optimized for deployment in low-resource and underserved settings.

## Open and Interoperable Architecture
Built using open technologies and designed for integration with existing health information systems.

---

# Why This Matters

Many vulnerable communities still rely on fragmented and reactive systems for managing climate-sensitive health risks. Delayed response often increases the impact of disease outbreaks and environmental emergencies, especially for children.

This platform aims to support a shift from reactive response toward anticipatory and data-driven action.

# Demo Dataset

This repository includes a synthetic climate-health dataset created for demonstration and prototyping purposes.

The dataset includes:
- rainfall indicators,
- temperature,
- humidity,
- flood risk levels,
- malaria risk scores,
- cholera risk scores,
- air quality indicators,
- and health facility alert simulations.

No real patient or sensitive data is included.
